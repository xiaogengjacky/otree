In this experiment, you will make a series of decisions over several rounds. In each round, you will receive an amount of points as your budget, and be shown a lottery. You will then have to go through the followings sequence of decisions in each round.

1. Determining the minimum sure payoff to accept: After you see the lottery, you will be asked to specify a minimum amount that you would accept in exchange of the lottery. Note that, the lottery could pay either a positive or a negative amount. In the case where the lottery paying a negative amount, you will have to specify the minimum sure loss that you would accept in exchange of a possible loss provided by the lottery.

2. Determining whether you will receive the lottery or the sure payoff: After you have decided the minimum amount you would accept in exchange of the lottery, the computer will generate a random amount that is larger than the lowest payoff of the lottery but smaller than the highest payoff of the lottery. If this random amount is greater than or equal to your minimum sure payoff amount that you are willing to accept in exchange of the lottery, you will receive this random amount as your payoff in that particular round. If this random amount is smaller than your minimum sure payoff amount that you are willing to accept in exchange of the lottery, you will keep the lottery. The computer will then determine the outcome of the lottery using a random draw according to the chance (probability) specified in that lottery.

At the end of the experiment, one round will be randomly picked and your payoff from the picked round will be your final payoff from this experiment. Since each round has an equal chance of being picked, it is in your best interest to treat every round as if it has been picked.
