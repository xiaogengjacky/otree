{% blocktrans trimmed with n=Constants.num_choices group_size=Constants.group_size num_lists=Constants.num_rounds %} You will have an initial balance of {{ endowment }} to start the experiment. In the following, you'll complete a series of group tasks. There will be {{group_size}} members in your group (including yourself). You and each member in your group will face {{ n }} decisions listed on each of your screens. On each screen, you will make decisions for your group by choosing either "Option A" or Option B in each row. At the end of this task, each member in your group will receive exactly the same amount. When we determine the group payment, one of the group member's decision will be randomly selected. The probability that your decision will be selected is {{ weight }}. On each screen you will make {{ n }} decisions and record these in the middle column. There will be a total of {{ num_lists }} screens (lists). {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans trimmed with hi=lottery_hi lo=lottery_lo %} On each screen "Option A" is a lottery that pays {{ hi }} with certain probabilities and {{ lo }} otherwise; "Option B" yields a varying payoff for sure, i.e. with a probability of 100%. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans trimmed with n=Constants.num_choices num_lists=Constants.num_rounds%} After you and your group members have all made decisions for all screens, one of the screens (lists) will be randomly picked by the computer for payment. For all the paired choices you made on that picked screen, one of the {{ n }} decisions will be randomly chosen for your group's payment. One choice out of all choices made by you and your group members will be randomly selected to be the chosen choice for your group.If your group, by randomly selecting one member's choice, opts for "Option A" in this decision, it will be randomly determined whether the low or high payoff will constitute your group’s payoff; if your group, by randomly selecting one member's choice, opts for "Option B", the respective sure amount will constitute your group’s payoff. The group payoff will then be divided equally among the {{ group_size }} members which will also be the individual payoff for each member. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans trimmed with n=num_choices num_lists=Constants.num_rounds%} Even though you will make {{ n }} votes on each of the {{ num_lists }} screens, only one of the screen and one decision in that screen will end up affecting your and group members' earnings, but you will not know in advance which decision will be used. Each decision has an equal chance of being used in the end. {% endblocktrans %}


{% blocktrans trimmed with n=num_choices %} To summarize: You will make {{ n }} choices on each screen; for each decision you will have to choose between "Option A" and "Option B" while bearing in mind that there is a {{ weight }} chance that your decision will determine everyone's payoff in your group. You may choose A for some decision rows and B for other rows. When your group is finished, one screen will be randomly chosen and one of the {{ n }} made by all group members decisions on that screen will be randomly picked for your group's payoff. If your group decided for "Option A", a random number will be drawn to determine your earnings; if your group decided for "Option B" you'll receive the respective sure amount. All members in the same group will receive exactly the same amount which is the group payoff divided by {{ group_size }}. {% endblocktrans %}

Please note that your identity and decisions for the group will be revealed to your group members at the end of this experiment.
